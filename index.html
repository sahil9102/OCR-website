  • OCR, or Optical Character Recognition, is a technology that enables computers to recognize and extract text from images, scanned documents, or handwritten documents and convert it into editable and searchable text. It plays a crucial role in digitizing printed or handwritten text, making it accessible, editable, and searchable by computers.
Our Process



Image Preprocessing

Before OCR begins, the image containing text undergoes preprocessing. This may involve tasks such as image enhancement, noise reduction, binarization (converting the image to black and white), and skew correction (straightening the text if it's tilted).

Text Detection

OCR algorithms identify regions within the image that contain text. This involves distinguishing text from other elements in the image, such as graphics or background noise.

Character Recognition

OCR software analyzes the segmented characters and matches them to known patterns or shapes. This process involves complex algorithms such as pattern recognition, machine learning, and neural networks to identify characters accurately despite variations in fonts, styles, and quality of the text.

Post-Processing

After text recognition, OCR software may perform post-processing tasks to improve the accuracy of the extracted text. This includes tasks such as spell-checking, correcting errors, and formatting the text to enhance readability.
